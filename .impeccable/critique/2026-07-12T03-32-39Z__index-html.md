## Anti-Patterns Verdict

**Does this look AI-generated? No overall — a bespoke hero rescues it — but the mid-page coasts.**

**LLM assessment (A)**: The hero is the most human-authored part of the page: "Player One · Des Moines, IA" with an attract-mode blinking dot, "I am / Snow" in a phosphor CRT glow (not the tired gradient-clip), a rotating conic marquee ring, and two hidden-toy hint chips. Specific, diegetic, opinionated. **But the personality does not survive the fold.** From Projects down it reverts to textbook composition: a 3-across glass card grid, then an icon-tile Stack grid, each section led by the same 0.9rem/60%-white intro + one lit underline. Nothing below the hero reads as a deliberate human choice. Verdict: not slop overall; mid-page is slop-adjacent by sameness.

**Deterministic scan (B — `detect.mjs`, 3 findings, all downgraded)**:
- [warning] Em-dash overuse "9 in body text" → **mostly false positive**: only **3** are in rendered copy (one section-intro, two CTA lines); the other 6 live in an HTML comment, `<meta>`/OG/Twitter tags, and an `img alt` — invisible to readers. Not a real cadence problem.
- [advisory] `border-radius: 2px` (l.105) → **false positive**: the 3px section-title marquee bar, a hairline accent, not a card.
- [advisory] `rgb(0 0 0 / .42)` (l.130) → **false positive**: a black drop-shadow alpha on card hover, not a palette color.
- Net: **no true structural/palette violations.** The substantive issues below came from the design + accessibility review, not the detector.

**Visual overlays**: live overlay injection skipped (static screenshot pipeline already produced the reveal-gating + blank-section evidence). No user-visible overlay this run.

## Overall Impression

The hero genuinely earns the "that site was FUN" reaction PRODUCT.md is chasing — it's the best thing on the page. Two gaps hold it back: **(1) the delight is front-loaded** — the toys that are supposed to be the memory are demoted to two low-contrast, keyboard-invisible chips, and the page below the hero drops to generic-portfolio grammar; **(2) accessibility of the pitch itself** — the one-line tagline and the toy hints are the lowest-contrast strings on the page, and the color picker can drive accent text below any legible floor. Biggest single opportunity: make the toys and the arcade voice survive past the first fold, and fix the contrast on the exact strings that carry the pitch.

## What's Working

1. **The hero's arcade diegetics are the real thing.** `attractBlink`, `crtFlicker`, and the `ringSpin` conic ring encode the brand metaphor into motion — and each has a `prefers-reduced-motion` off-switch. "Design IS the product," delivered.
2. **Status surfacing is rich and honest.** The time bar (Snow's time vs. your detected time, live session timer, active-accent hex, TI-83 state) makes the site feel alive and responsive to *you* — reinforces the tinkerer positioning with zero marketing copy.
3. **CTA hierarchy matches strategy.** "Grab the Résumé ↗" is the solid primary, GitHub the ghost fallback — résumé is PRODUCT.md's primary CTA and now reads that way at a glance.

## Priority Issues

- **[P1] The pitch text fails WCAG AA contrast.** `.hero-tagline` is `rgba(var(--accent-rgb),.75)` ≈ **3.3:1** on Void at weight 300 italic; `.hero-hint` chips are accent at `.6` ≈ **2.5:1** at 0.72rem. The two lowest-contrast strings on the page are the one-line pitch and the only signposts to the toys. **Fix**: tagline → solid white ≥.7 (or accent ≥.9) at weight 400; hints → near-white label text, lean on border/bg. Verify ≥4.5:1. *(Note: white-at-opacity body text is fine — `.6` white ≈ 7.3:1 passes; the problem is specifically accent-colored text on Void.)* → **$impeccable harden**
- **[P1] Hero toy-hints are keyboard-inaccessible.** Both are `<span class="hero-hint" onclick=…>` with no `tabindex`/`role`/key handler; TI-83 + terminal are unreachable without a mouse — the page's delight payload is invisible to keyboard/AT users. **Fix**: make them `<button>` (or `role="button"` + `tabindex="0"` + Enter/Space); they already have focus-ring styles. → **$impeccable harden**
- **[P1] Content is gated behind `.reveal{opacity:0}` + IntersectionObserver.** B confirmed: without a real scroll the un-forced desktop/mobile renders show Projects, Stack, and CTA **blank** — the observer never fires. Only the `prefers-reduced-motion` block force-shows content; a normal-motion user whose JS/observer fails (or a prerender/locked-down browser) sees empty sections where the proof of "capable" should be. **Fix**: default content visible; make `reveal` a progressive enhancement added only when JS is present. → **$impeccable harden**
- **[P2] 8-item nav, no skip-link.** Projects · Stack · Analytics · Schedule · Contact · 📷 Photography · ✍️ Guestbook · ⌨️ Terminal — 8 inline links (9 with 🎨/TI-83), mixing in-page anchors with cross-page destinations and no visual separation; keyboard users tab all of them with no skip link. **Fix**: group the three standalone toys under one "Playground" entry; add a focus-revealed skip-to-content link. → **$impeccable layout**
- **[P2] The color picker can make the pitch unreadable.** Hero greeting, tagline, hints, section underlines, and stack labels are all `var(--accent-rgb)`; a user-chosen dark/desaturated accent (fully supported by the hex + hue sliders) can drive them to ~1.5:1. A headline feature can silently break the pitch. **Fix**: clamp chosen accents to a minimum luminance for *text* use, or route accent-on-Void text through a lightened derived token while keeping the raw accent for glows/borders. → **$impeccable harden**
- **[P3] Below-the-fold doesn't inherit the hero's personality.** Projects (3-col glass grid) → Stack (icon-tile grid), each with the same grey intro + underline — the generic-portfolio anti-reference the redesign set out to avoid, just relocated below the fold. **Fix**: carry one arcade motif down — a cabinet-marquee Projects header, a "high-score" framing on Stack tiles, a scanline/CRT hover pushing the `.project-card::before` lit rule further. → **$impeccable bolder**

## Persona Red Flags

**Jordan (first-timer)**: Meets an 8-item nav mixing "Projects" (scrolls) with "⌨️ Terminal" (navigates away) with no cue which is which — clicks Terminal expecting a section, gets bounced off the homepage. The "press [MODE] to unlock something" chip is a low-contrast span with no affordance, so Jordan never finds the toy.

**Riley (stress-tester)**: Keyboard-tabs immediately → the toy hints can't be focused or activated, and there's no skip link so every Tab run starts with 8 nav stops. Opens 🎨, sets a near-black accent, and watches the tagline, hints, and underlines vanish with no guard — a reproducible "I broke your site." Also: throttle JS / background the tab during load and the reveal-gated sections never appear.

**Nadia (recruiter — brand-specific)**: Here for signal in ~20 seconds. Résumé CTA correctly primary — good. But the pitch line she needs ("I build the tools Discord communities rely on") is 300-weight italic purple at ~3.3:1 and easy to skim past; Projects intro is generic grey; and Analytics/Schedule/Contact — the sections that substantiate "capable" — depend entirely on external SVGs/JS with no static fallback, so on a flaky network or a locked-down corporate browser she may see empty gaps where the proof should be.

## Questions to Consider

1. If the toys are the thing people should remember, why are they behind the two lowest-contrast, keyboard-invisible strings on the page — what changes if a playable TI-83 or terminal *previews itself* in the hero instead of being a hint?
2. The hero screams "arcade" and everything below whispers "SaaS template" — delete the hero, and is anything left recognizably *Snow's*?
3. You built a picker that lets a visitor repaint the identity — is that empowering play, or handing every recruiter a way to make your pitch text unreadable before they've read it?
