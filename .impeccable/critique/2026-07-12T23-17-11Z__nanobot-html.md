## Anti-Patterns Verdict

**AI-made? No — the hero + killer-feature framing carry a real POV; the 13-tile secondary grid is where it flirts with template.**

- **Design review (A)**: Escapes slop on three counts — real mascot art (not gradient headline text), a genuinely differentiated "No More Copying IDs" callout (not "fast/secure/powerful" filler), and specific load-bearing copy (`/last`, `n!tagname`, real counts). The two-tier feature hierarchy (lit flagship trio vs. denser secondary) is the single most important decision and it's correct. Weakest zone: the **13 near-identical secondary tiles**, emoji-per-card, ragged bottom edges, and the gimmick "0ms" stat.
- **Detector (B)**: 32 findings, **nearly all false-positive** — 11 font flags are the intentional Bricolage/Hanken sub-brand (the one real action: document them in DESIGN.md), 17 size flags are this surface's own type scale, the color flag is a shadow alpha, the 2px radius is a hairline bar. **Two true-but-minor**: `.nb-callout-cmd` (6px) and `.nb-cmd` (8px) chip radii sit off the scale. No structural/a11y antipattern fired.
- **Overlays**: injection skipped; computed-style + screenshots carried the evidence.

## Overall Impression

The top of the page is genuinely good — mascot hero + "No More Copying IDs" lands the aha in the first ~10 seconds, exactly right for a server owner's snap decision. Two things undercut it: **the conversion path evaporates** (the closing CTA sells Top.gg, not "Add to Server," so a reader persuaded by tile 6 has no button nearby and none at the bottom), and the **13-tile wall** is unranked and ragged. Biggest single win: put "Add NanoBot to your server" back as the primary action at the bottom (and ideally sticky on mobile).

## What's Working

1. **The two-tier feature hierarchy is a real art-direction call** — the lit flagship trio vs. the denser secondary grid gives the eye an entry point and keeps 16 features from reading as one mass.
2. **The killer-feature framing is positioning, not filler** — a full marquee callout for "No More Copying IDs" + the `/last` chip picks one differentiator and sells it.
3. **Real mascot art + Discord-native voice** — the rendered character carries the hero (no gradient text), and the Slash/Prefix/Mention bar speaks the audience's language.

## Priority Issues

- **[P1] The conversion action disappears at the bottom.** The closing `.nb-cta` ("Want to dig in?") makes **Top.gg the blue primary** and never repeats "Add to Server." After 16 tiles the persuaded reader must scroll back to the hero to convert — the biggest leak on the page (also violates peak-end). **Fix**: make the bottom CTA "Add NanoBot to your server" (same OAuth href) the blue primary; demote Top.gg/GitHub/Docs to ghosts; consider a sticky "Add to Server" on the long mobile scroll. → **$impeccable clarify** / structural edit
- **[P1] `Servers: --` shows a placeholder as a real value.** For a server owner, install count is the trust signal; a dead dash reads "nobody uses this / broken." (Local `--` is an env limitation, but the fallback behavior ships.) **Fix**: hide the Servers stat when the count is unknown, or render a sane fallback — never show `--` where a number is promised. → **$impeccable harden**
- **[P2] Mobile hero touch targets are under 44px.** Measured at 390px: "Add to Server" = **38px** tall, "Support Server" = **40px** — both under the guideline, on the page whose whole thesis is mobile. **Fix**: bump hero `.btn` vertical padding on small screens to ≥44px. → **$impeccable harden**
- **[P2] Small text fails WCAG AA contrast.** Confirmed ratios on Void: `.nb-cat` section labels **4.06:1**, cookie-banner link **4.06:1**, and stat sublabels (white `.4`) **3.74:1** — all normal-size. (The `.4/.45/.5` accent alphas that look worst are `border-color`, not text — fine.) **Fix**: raise stat sublabels to ≥.6 white; bump `.nb-cat` / cookie-link lightness to clear 4.5:1. → **$impeccable harden**
- **[P2] The 13-tile secondary grid is ragged and unranked.** `auto-fill minmax(255px)` pairs cards of very different copy length (Live Role 2 lines vs. Music 6) → uneven bottoms; all 13 carry equal weight with no "start here." **Fix**: clamp descriptions to ~3 lines with a min-height, and/or sub-group the 13 into 2–3 labeled clusters (Safety / Engagement / Utility) so each scan set is ≤5. → **$impeccable layout**
- **[P3] "0ms Setup Time" reads as a gimmick.** An impossible stat beside real numbers (368 commands) makes the honest ones look softer. **Fix**: relabel to "No setup" / "Instant" / "< 1 min". → **$impeccable clarify**

## Persona Red Flags

**Dara (server owner, ~10s decision — the real audience)**: Hero converts her fast, but `Servers: --` denies her the one number she wants, and if she's sold mid-scroll there's no "Add to Server" nearby and none at the bottom — the page wins attention then makes converting harder.

**Casey (distracted mobile)**: Mobile-first promise delivered (clean column, real mascot, legible type) — but it's a very long single-column scroll with no sticky CTA, hero buttons under 44px, and the cookie banner overlaps the input-method chips on first paint.

**Jordan (first-timer)**: Mostly fine — the Slash/Prefix/Mention bar preempts "what's `n!`?". Minor: the raw OAuth permissions link grants broad perms with no "here's what NanoBot can access" reassurance near the button.

## Minor Observations

- **Purple→blue flash on load**: blue is applied in a `DOMContentLoaded` handler *after* `theme.min.js` (defer) sets the amethyst default — a real accent FOUC on slow loads. Set the blue default earlier/inline to kill it.
- The count-up `Servers` stat animation isn't covered by the `prefers-reduced-motion` block (only the mascot float + attract blink are).
- Detector's 30 font/size flags are the intentional sub-brand — the one real follow-up is documenting Bricolage/Hanken in DESIGN.md so the scanner (and future work) sees them as sanctioned.
- 16 full-color emoji icons are the loudest non-brand color on an otherwise disciplined blue/glass palette — acceptable for the playful voice, but they fight the accent slightly.

## Questions to Consider

1. You spend a full callout convincing Dara that NanoBot solves her #1 pain — so why is the button that acts on it only at the very top, and replaced by "vote on Top.gg" where her intent peaks?
2. If the Servers count is your strongest trust signal, is showing `--` on failure worse than hiding the stat entirely?
3. The two-tier split proves you can rank features — so why do all 13 secondary tiles carry identical weight, when half are whole product categories (Economy, Leveling, Music) a mod-focused buyer may not want?
