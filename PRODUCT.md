# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

Three audiences across one domain (snowbuilds.dev). The portfolio (index and its satellite pages — photography, guestbook, terminal) speaks primarily to recruiters and hiring managers deciding whether to reach out, and secondarily to fellow developers who enjoy poking at the toys. The NanoBot landing page speaks to Discord server owners deciding in seconds whether to add the bot. The NanoBot docs serve people already using or self-hosting the bot and need clarity over spectacle.

## Product Purpose

A personal portfolio that doubles as a live demo of its author's skill, plus a home for NanoBot. For the portfolio, success is a visitor reaching out with an opportunity. For NanoBot, success is growth: the landing page converts a curious server owner into an install, and docs so easy that users recommend the bot to friends.

## Positioning

A personality-first developer: the site itself — every hand-built toy, easter egg, and interaction — is the resume.

## Conversion & proof

- Primary and secondary CTA: primary is grabbing the resume (resume.pdf); the fallback is exploring — play with the terminal, the TI-83, the color picker, sign the guestbook. For NanoBot, primary is adding the bot to a server, fallback is reading the docs.
- The line a visitor remembers after 10 seconds: "That site was FUN."
- Belief ladder: (1) this is delightful → (2) I want to know how it works → (3) this person is genuinely capable.
- Proof on hand: live shipped projects — NanoBot running in real servers, the deployed site itself with its working D1-backed guestbook, GitHub commit history, and github-metrics.svg.

## Brand Personality

Playful, curious, hands-on. The voice invites the visitor to touch things rather than telling them the author is skilled. Warm and personal, never corporate; clever, never edgy for its own sake.

## Anti-references

- Generic template portfolios: hero + skill bars + identical card grid + contact form, indistinguishable from every bootcamp graduate's site.
- Corporate SaaS landing pages: Stripe/Linear-clone gradients, stat rows, enterprise polish.
- Overdone hacker aesthetic: Matrix rain, neon-green mono everywhere, dark-web edginess for its own sake.

## Design Principles

1. **Play is the pitch.** Every toy is a competence demo in disguise — delight on first touch, depth on inspection.
2. **Let them touch it.** Prefer interactive over static; things on this site respond, react, and reward curiosity.
3. **Show the shipping.** Credibility comes from live, working things (the guestbook works, the bot is in real servers), never from claims or skill bars.
4. **Fun never blocks the exit.** The resume and contact are always one obvious step away; playfulness never costs a conversion or an accessibility requirement.
5. **One voice, three volumes.** Portfolio turns personality up; the NanoBot landing focuses it into conversion; the docs turn it down in favor of clarity — but it is recognizably the same site throughout.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA: 4.5:1 body-text contrast (3:1 for large text), full keyboard navigability, and a `prefers-reduced-motion` alternative for every animation. Toys and easter eggs must degrade gracefully — no content or CTA locked behind an interaction that requires a pointer or motion.
